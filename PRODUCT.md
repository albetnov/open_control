# Product

## Register

product

## Platform

android

## Users
A solo streamer/content creator who runs OBS Studio on a desktop and wants to control it remotely from a phone while live — checking status, reconnecting, and adjusting scenes without leaving the camera or walking back to the desk.

## Product Purpose
A personal, local-network remote control for OBS Studio over obs-websocket (v5, default port 4455). Connects a phone directly to the desktop instance and reconnects quickly to previously used setups, with no cloud account or relay involved. Success looks like: open the app, get connected in one or two taps, and never have to touch the desktop mid-stream.

## Positioning
A local-network-only OBS remote built around one exact streaming setup, not a general-purpose configurable tool — direct device-to-desktop WebSocket control, no account, no cloud relay.

## Brand Personality
Calm, minimal remote-control energy in the spirit of Apple Home, Sonos, and Nanoleaf: few controls, instant status, nothing to configure once it's set up. The voice is quiet and utilitarian, never chatty.

## Anti-references
Generic SaaS dashboards — no cards-everywhere layouts, no gradient stat tiles, no admin-template look.

## Design Principles
Local-first, no ceremony: every flow favors direct control over indirection, with no account gating, cloud roundtrips, or unnecessary confirmation steps. Glanceable in low light: legible and operable at a glance next to a monitor without full attention, since this is a mid-stream tool, not a destination app. Built for one setup, not every setup: optimized for the user's own OBS instance rather than generic configurability, while still covering the ordinary states (disconnected, reconnecting, failed). Calm remote, not a mixer console: a small number of confident controls beats a dense control-surface panel.

## Accessibility & Inclusion
Dark, low-light-friendly by default, since it's used beside a monitor/desk while streaming. Standard contrast and tap-target accessibility baseline; no other requirements stated.
